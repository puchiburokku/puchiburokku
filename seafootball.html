Friends of The Sea Series

Football Fish

August 15th, 2024, 11:50 am

Is it a fish or a football?...Honestly, who gives a fish when it looks as cool as this!!

 

The Unboxing: Quality and First Impressions

After falling in love with Daiso’s Dinosaur Friends Series, I decided to dive more to the cool types of models; their Friends of the Sea series: The Football Fish. It..is quite a quirky and slightly spooky marine creature with its iconic glowing lure—immediately caught my eye and honestly I didn't know it's name was "Football Fish" haha. The kit promised many movable parts in it's intricate build so I was quite excited.

Opening the package, I was greeted by multiple hues of chocolate and yellow blocks and a few white cylindrical blocks that represents the fish's teeth. The instructions, as always, were concise but clear, breaking down the Football Fish into manageable steps.

Building the Football Fish

The Football Fish wasn’t just another block build—it was a deep-sea adventure! The process started with the fish’s body, a rounded, layered design that required careful stacking to achieve its bulbous shape. This was quite different from the straight, angular builds I’d done in the past.

As I worked, I discovered just how important alignment was. A single misaligned piece could throw off the fish’s curvature, so I double-checked each layer before moving on. The fins and mouth were another highlight; THEY CAN MOVE! but anyway, while piecing the fins and mouth together, it was so hard to lock it in place that my fingers accidentally slipped and threw the pieces off the table haha XD

The blocks themselves were sturdy and clicked together perfectly, which is one of the things I’ve come to love about Daiso’s Petite Blocks. However, this set had more movable parts and irregular shapes and angles than the typical models, making it a bit more advanced and perfect for seasoned builders looking for a challenge.

Therefore, I give it a full 3 out of 3 stars in difficulty!

My Tips!

1. Sort Your Pieces: Sorting your blocks by color and size saves time and makes following the instructions much easier.

2. Work in Stages: Complete one section (like the paws or tail) before moving to the next. This keeps you focused and reduces the chance of mistakes.

3. Take Breaks: The irregular shape can feel tricky at times. Don’t hesitate to pause and step back if you’re feeling stuck—it’s better than forcing pieces into the wrong spots.
